Data from The Onion's "American Voices" series

 

 

 

Since at least 1996, The Onion has run a series of fake person-on-the-street interviews called American Voices, which has made me laugh often enough that I decided to collect the responses in a spreadsheet. Enjoy!

The data is pretty straightforward: Each row is one response to a prompt. As of August 2023, there were ~23,000 responses to ~7,200 prompts.

The article_id column can be used as a unique identifier for each prompt in addition to the page URL. The picture_category column has the numeric ID I gave to each picture of a fake respondent. (You can see a list of pictures tied to these IDs in the picture_key worksheet.)
